The '''2025 Stanley Cup Final''' was the [[Stanley Cup Final|championship series]] of the [[National Hockey League]]'s (NHL) [[2024–25 NHL season|2024–25 season]] and the culmination of the [[2025 Stanley Cup playoffs]]. In a rematch of [[2024 Stanley Cup Final|the previous year's Final]], the [[Eastern Conference (NHL)|Eastern Conference]] and defending [[Stanley Cup]] champion [[2024–25 Florida Panthers season|Florida Panthers]] defeated the [[Western Conference (NHL)|Western Conference]] champion [[2024–25 Edmonton Oilers season|Edmonton Oilers]] four games to two in the [[best-of-seven]] series, earning their second consecutive championship, as well as the second title in team history.

The series began on June 4 and concluded on June 17.<ref name="schedule">{{cite web |title=2025 Stanley Cup Final schedule |url=https://www.nhl.com/news/2025-stanley-cup-final-schedule-television-results |access-date=May 29, 2025 |website=[[National Hockey League]]}}</ref> Edmonton had [[Home advantage|home ice advantage]] in the series due to having the better regular season record. This was the sixth consecutive Final to feature a team from [[Florida]]. This was the first time since {{scfy|2009}} that the Final featured a rematch of the previous season, and the first time since 1978 that the rematch was won by the same team as the year before. It was also the first time since {{scfy|2012}} that neither finalist had home-ice advantage in the first three rounds.

==Paths to the Final==
===Edmonton Oilers===
{{Main|2024–25 Edmonton Oilers season}}
This was Edmonton's second consecutive and ninth overall Final appearance. They have won the Stanley Cup five times, each between {{scfy|1984}} and {{scfy|1990}} during their [[Dynasty (sports)|dynasty years]]. Their most recent Stanley Cup win came in 1990 when they defeated the [[Boston Bruins]] in five games.

Edmonton finished third place in the [[Pacific Division (NHL)|Pacific Division]] during the regular season with a {{win-loss record|w=48|l=29|otl=5}} record. In the first round, they defeated their [[Kings–Oilers rivalry|intra-division rival]], the [[Los Angeles Kings]], in six games, eliminating them for the fourth consecutive season. They then defeated the division champion [[Vegas Golden Knights]] in five games during the second round. In the Western Conference final, they triumphed over the [[Dallas Stars]] for the second consecutive season, winning in five games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/edmonton-oilers-game-by-game-path-to-2025-stanley-cup-final|title=Oilers game-by-game recap on way to Cup Final|last=Satriano|first=David|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. ===Florida Panthers===
{{Main|2024–25 Florida Panthers season}}

This was Florida's third consecutive and fourth overall Final appearance. They have won one Stanley Cup, doing so the [[2024 Stanley Cup Final|previous season]] against the Oilers in seven games after giving up a 3–0 series lead.

During the offseason, in free agency, the Panthers acquired forwards [[Jesper Boqvist]], [[A. J. Greer]], and [[Tomáš Nosek|Tomas Nosek]], as well as defenceman [[Nate Schmidt]]. They also re-signed forwards [[Sam Reinhart]], [[Anton Lundell]], and [[Carter Verhaeghe]], along with defenceman [[Dmitry Kulikov (ice hockey)|Dmitry Kulikov]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.miamiherald.com/sports/nhl/florida-panthers/article289656872.html|title=Panthers free agency: Who's staying, who's coming and who's gone? Full list of Day 1 moves|last=McPherson|first=Jordan|newspaper=Miami Herald|date=July 1, 2024|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/41690892/carter-verhaeghe-panthers-agree-8-year-56m-deal|title=Carter Verhaeghe, Panthers agree to 8-year, $56M deal|last=Wyshnyski|first=Greg|publisher=ESPN Enterprises, Inc.|date=October 8, 2024|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref> Nearing the trade deadline, the team acquired defenceman [[Seth Jones]] from the [[Chicago Blackhawks]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/44072940/panthers-acquire-veteran-seth-jones-blackhawks|title=Florida Panthers acquire Seth Jones from Chicago Blackhawks|last=Wyshnyski|first=Greg|publisher=ESPN Enterprises, Inc.|date=March 1, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref> as well as goaltender [[Vítek Vaněček|Vitek Vanecek]] and forward [[Nico Sturm]] from the [[San Jose Sharks]] in separate transactions.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/44122525/panthers-add-goalie-vitek-vanecek-trade-sharks|title=Panthers add goalie Vitek Vanecek in trade with Sharks|publisher=ESPN Enterprises, Inc.|date=March 5, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/nhl/article/sharks-trade-f-nico-sturm-to-panthers-for-fourth-round-pick/|title=Sharks trade F Nico Sturm to Panthers for fourth-round pick|publisher=Rogers Media|date=March 6, 2025|website=Sportsnet.ca|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref> On the day of the trade deadline, the team traded for [[Boston Bruins]] captain [[Brad Marchand]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/nhl-panthers-bruins-trade-marchand-349eb843e33d6565ceb3507452495cab|title=Florida Panthers add veteran Brad Marchand as they gear up for run at another Stanley Cup title|last=Thames|first=Alanis|publisher=Associated Press|date=March 7, 2025|website=APNews.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref>

Reinhart led the team in scoring with 81 points.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/panthers/news/injury-reinhart-exits-game-2-after-hit-from-aho|title=Reinhart exits Game 2 after hit from Aho|last=Olive|first=Jameson|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=May 22, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref> Team captain [[Aleksander Barkov]] won his third [[Frank J. Selke Trophy]] as the NHL's best defensive forward.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.reuters.com/sports/panthers-c-aleksander-barkov-repeats-selke-winner-2025-06-02/|title=Panthers C Aleksander Barkov repeats as Selke winner|publisher=Reuters|date=June 2, 2025|website=Reuters.com|access-date=June 4, 2025}}</ref>

Florida finished third place in the [[Atlantic Division (NHL)|Atlantic Division]] during the regular season with a {{win-loss record|w=47|l=31|otl=4}} record. In the first round, they defeated their [[Lightning–Panthers rivalry|intra-state rivals]], the [[Tampa Bay Lightning]], in five games. They then defeated the division champion [[Toronto Maple Leafs]] in seven games during the second round, despite initially trailing 2–0 and 3–2 in the series. In the Eastern Conference final, they eliminated the [[Carolina Hurricanes]] for the second time in three seasons, winning in five games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/florida-panthers-path-to-2025-stanley-cup-final|title=Panthers game-by-game recap on way to Stanley Cup Final|last=Satriano|first=David|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. [[File:Carterverhaeghe2023.png|thumb|right|upright=.7|[[Carter Verhaeghe]] recorded the game-winning goal and an assist in game three.]]
Game three began as game two ended with [[Brad Marchand]] scoring 56 seconds into the first period.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/video/brad-marchand-goal-vs-edmonton-002838139.html|title=Brad Marchand with a Goal vs. Edmonton Oilers|publisher=Yahoo|date=June 9, 2025|website=Sports.Yahoo.com|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> The Panthers made it 2–0 on the power play as [[Evan Rodrigues]] made the pass to [[Carter Verhaeghe]] who wristed it past Oilers goaltender [[Stuart Skinner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/video/clip?id=45482260|title=Carter Verhaeghe goes top shelf for Panthers goal|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 9, 2025|website=ESPN.co.uk|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> In the second period, a shot by [[Evan Bouchard]] was deflected by [[Sergei Bobrovsky]] to [[Corey Perry]] who cut the goal deficit for the Oilers to 2–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/video/edm-fla-perry-scores-goal-against-sergei-bobrovsky-6374070037112|title=Perry's PPG cuts the deficit|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 9, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> Forward [[Sam Reinhart]] restored the two-goal lead for Florida, taking the pass from Verhaeghe.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/video/clip?id=45482404|title=Sam Reinhart gives Panthers instant response|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 9, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> The Panthers then made it 4–1 when a two-on-zero rush allowed [[Sam Bennett (ice hockey)|Sam Bennett]] to breakaway a wrist a shot past Skinner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/video/clip?id=45482513|title=Sam Bennett slots home Panthers' 4th goal|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 9, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> In the third period, Skinner attempted to clear the puck but did so over the glass giving Florida a power play. On the ensuing power play, [[Matthew Tkachuk]] made a pass to Reinhart who backhand-passed to [[Aaron Ekblad]] to make it 5–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/video/aaron-ekblad-powerplay-goal-vs-023316922.html|title=Aaron Ekblad with a Powerplay Goal vs. Edmonton Oilers|publisher=Yahoo|date=June 9, 2025|website=Sports.Yahoo.com|access-date=June 9, 2025}}</ref> Skinner was pulled after this goal. [[File:Vasily Podkolzin, October 2021 (cropped) (2).jpg|thumb|right|upright=.7|[[Vasily Podkolzin]] (pictured with the [[Vancouver Canucks]]) scored the game-tying goal and assisted on the overtime winner in game four.]]
The Oilers came back from a three-goal deficit in game four to emerge victorious in overtime. In the first period, with both [[Evander Kane]] and [[Darnell Nurse]] in the penalty box for separate infractions, Florida captain [[Aleksander Barkov]] off the faceoff passed to [[Matthew Tkachuk]] whose snap shot beat [[Stuart Skinner]] to make it 1–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/video/edm-fla-tkachuk-scores-ppg-against-stuart-skinner-6374212010112|title=Tkachuk kicks off scoring with PPG|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 12, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> Tkachuk made it 2–0 on another power play, picking up a rebound from [[Sam Reinhart]]'s shot on Skinner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/panthers/video/fla-vs-edm-tkachuk-strikes-again-6374210440112|title=FLA vs. EDM: Tkachuk strikes again|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 12, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> Towards the end of the first period, [[Carter Verhaeghe]] picked up a loose puck in the offensive zone sending it back to [[Anton Lundell]] who fired a wrist shot past Skinner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/video/clip?id=45502618|title=Panthers go up 3-0 on Anton Lundell's 1st-period goal|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 12, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> In the second period, the Oilers got on the power play and with [[Leon Draisaitl]]'s pass to [[Ryan Nugent-Hopkins]], Edmonton scored to cut the deficit to two goals.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/video/ryan-nugent-hopkins-powerplay-goal-013138079.html|title=Ryan Nugent-Hopkins with a Powerplay Goal vs. Florida Panthers|publisher=Yahoo|date=June 12, 2025|website=Sports.Yahoo.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> Midway through the second, in the Panthers defensive zone, [[Mattias Ekholm]] passed to [[Darnell Nurse]] whose shot at a tight angle got past [[Sergei Bobrovsky]] to cut the deficit to one goal.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.co.uk/video/clip?id=45502871|title=Darnell Nurse scores from impossible angle for Oilers|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 12, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> The Oilers tied it when [[Vasily Podkolzin]] backhand shot the puck past Bobrovsky.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/video/edm-fla-podkolzin-scores-goal-against-sergei-bobrovsky-6374213192112|title=Podkolzin evens score|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 12, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> In the third period, after the Panthers turned over the puck, [[Kasperi Kapanen]] passed to [[Jake Walman]] who scored to make it 4–3 for Edmonton.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/nhl/video/gotta-see-it-oilers-walman-hammers-home-missile-from-the-point/|title=Gotta See It: Oilers' Walman hammers home missile from the dot|publisher=Rogers Media|date=June 12, 2025|website=Sportsnet.ca|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> With under a minute left in the third period, the Panthers pulled Bobrovsky for an extra skater. With 20 seconds remaining, Reinhart scored for Florida to send it to overtime.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/video/clip/_/id/45503306|title=Sam Reinhart's huge goal sends Game 4 into overtime|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 12, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> In overtime, Draisatl while holding off a Florida defenceman, made a backhand shot that went between Bobrovsky.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/watch/player/_/id/45503512|title=Leon Draisaitl scores OT winner for Oilers in Game 4|publisher=ESPN Enterprises Inc.|date=June 12, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> He scored his fourth overtime goal of the playoffs, setting the record for most overtime goals scored in one season.<ref>{{cite web|title=Playoff Skater Records|url=https://records.nhl.com/records/playoff-skater-records/overtime/most-overtime-goals-one-season-playoff|website=records.nhl.com|access-date=June 13, 2025}}</ref> He also became the first player since [[John LeClair]] in {{scfy|1993}} to score two overtime goals in the same year in the Final.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/nhl/article/oilers-draisaitl-sets-nhl-record-for-most-overtime-goals-in-single-post-season/|title=Oilers' Draisaitl sets NHL record for most overtime goals in single post-season|publisher=Rogers Media|date=June 12, 2025|website=Sportsnet.ca|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/edmonton-oilers-florida-panthers-game-4-recap|title=Draisaitl lifts Oilers past Panthers in OT for Game 4 win to even Stanley Cup Final|last=Rosen|first=Dan|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 12, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 12, 2025}}</ref> It was the seventh time a team came back from a three-goal deficit in the Final and the first since {{scfy|2006}}.<ref>{{cite news|date=June 13, 2025|title=Oilers stun Panthers in biggest Stanley Cup final road comeback in 106 years|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2025/jun/12/edmonton-florida-stanley-cup-game-four-taylor-travis|access-date=June 13, 2025|newspaper=The Guardian|issn=0261-3077}}</ref>

With the extra attacker, [[Corey Perry]] fired a slap shot that went past Bobrovsky, giving Edmonton their second goal of the game.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/video/corey-perry-goal-vs-florida-024522396.html|title=Corey Perry with a Goal vs. Florida Panthers|publisher=Yahoo|date=June 14, 2025|website=Sports.Yahoo.com|access-date=June 15, 2025}}</ref> The Oilers pulled their goaltender again, but with the empty net, Luostarinen scored to give Florida a 5–2 lead, en route to a 3–2 series lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/florida-panthers-edmonton-oilers-game-5-recap-june-14|title=Marchand, Panthers top Oilers in Game 5 of Stanley Cup Final, 1 win from repeat|last=Rosen|first=Dan|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. ===Game six===

In game six, the Panthers began the scoring with [[Sam Reinhart]] picking up a defensive turnover by [[Evan Bouchard]] and snapping the puck past [[Stuart Skinner]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/video/edm-fla-reinhart-scores-goal-against-stuart-skinner-6374487829112|title=Reinhart pots slick opener in Game 6|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 17, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 17, 2025}}</ref> Florida doubled the lead as [[Eetu Luostarinen]] made a pass to [[Matthew Tkachuk]] whose wrist shot beat Skinner.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/video/edm-fla-tkachuk-scores-goal-against-stuart-skinner-6374489121112|title=Tkachuk doubles the lead|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 17, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 17, 2025}}</ref> In the second period, captain [[Aleksander Barkov]] assisted in making it 3–0 for the Panthers as he gathered a rebound off of Skinner's right shoulder and fed Reinhart who was standing near the crease. The puck ultimately deflected off Reinhart's skate and past Skinner to extend the lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://sports.yahoo.com/video/sam-reinhart-goal-vs-edmonton-015423784.html|title=Sam Reinhart with a Goal vs. Edmonton Oilers|publisher=Yahoo|date=June 17, 2025|website=Sports.Yahoo.com|access-date=June 17, 2025}}</ref> As the end of the game approached, the Oilers pulled their goaltender with less than seven minutes left. However, the Panthers recovered the puck and Reinhart shot into the empty net, scoring a [[hat-trick|hat trick]]. He would get his fourth goal of the match, hitting another empty net goal with just over five minutes remaining. Reinhart became the first player since [[Babe Dye]] in {{scfy|1922}} and the second player overall in league history to score four goals in a series-clinching game in the Stanley Cup Final. Reinhart also became the first player to score four goals in a Final game since [[Maurice Richard]] in {{scfy|1957}}. Edmonton would break the shutout as [[Vasily Podkolzin]] scored to make it 5–1. The Panthers defended their lead as [[Sergei Bobrovsky]] made 28 saves throughout the game, helping Florida earn their second Stanley Cup.<ref name="G6">{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/edmonton-oilers-florida-panthers-game-6-recap-june-17|title=Reinhart scores 4, Panthers top Oilers in Game 6 to repeat as Stanley Cup champions|last=Gulitti|first=Tom|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.|date=June 17, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 17, 2025}}</ref> Florida became the first team to defeat the same opponent in consecutive Final since the [[Montreal Canadiens]] defeated the [[Boston Bruins]] in {{scfy|1978}}.<ref name="G6" /> In addition, they are the third team to win the Stanley Cup without having home ice advantage in any round of the playoffs after the [[1994–95 New Jersey Devils season|1994–95 New Jersey Devils]] and the [[2011–12 Los Angeles Kings season|2011–12 Los Angeles Kings]].<ref>{{cite web|title=BY THE NUMBERS: Key stats for the Panthers in the Stanley Cup Final|url=https://www.nhl.com/panthers/news/by-the-numbers-key-stats-for-the-panthers-in-the-stanley-cup-final|last=Olive|first=Jameson|access-date=June 23, 2025|website=NHL.com|date=June 19, 2025}}</ref>

[[Sam Bennett (ice hockey)|Sam Bennett]] was awarded the [[Conn Smythe Trophy]] as MVP of the playoffs.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/45534912/panthers-bennett-wins-conn-smythe-playoff-best-15-goals|title=Bennett wins Conn Smythe after NHL-high 15 goals|last=Shilton|first=Kristen|date=June 18, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=June 23, 2025}}</ref>

==Media rights==
In Canada, this was the eleventh consecutive Stanley Cup Final broadcast by [[NHL on Sportsnet|Sportsnet]] and [[CBC Television]] in [[English language|English]], and [[TVA Sports]] in [[French language|French]]. The series was also streamed on [[Sportsnet#Sportsnet+|Sportsnet+]].<ref name="schedule"/><ref>{{cite web|last=Rosen|first=Dan|date=November 26, 2013|title=NHL, Rogers announce landmark 12-year deal|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/nhl-rogers-announce-landmark-12-year-deal/c-693152|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190620135912/https://www.nhl.com/news/nhl-rogers-announce-landmark-12-year-deal/c-693152|archive-date=June 20, 2019 |access-date=June 8, 2021|website=NHL.com|publisher=NHL Enterprises, L. P.}}</ref>

In the United States, the series was televised on [[NHL on TNT|TNT]] and simulcast on [[TruTV]]. This was the first time that the Final was available on [[HBO Max|Max]] since the service started streaming TNT Sports-produced NHL telecasts last season.<ref name="schedule"/><ref>{{cite press release |title=TNT Sports to Exclusively Present 2025 Stanley Cup Final — Florida Panthers vs. Edmonton Oilers — on TNT, truTV & Max |url=https://press.wbd.com/us/media-release/tnt-sports-exclusively-present-2025-stanley-cup-final-florida-panthers-vs-edmonton |website=wbd.com |date=June 2, 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Roth|first=Emma|date=September 19, 2023|title=Max will start offering a live sports tier in October|url=https://www.theverge.com/2023/9/19/23880831/max-live-sports-tier-launch-date-price|access-date=September 19, 2023|website=[[The Verge]]}}</ref> This was the fourth year of a seven-year deal in which [[NHL on ABC|ABC]] has the Final in even years and TNT has the series in odd years.<ref>{{cite web |last=Bucholtz |first=Andrew |title=First NHL playoffs under ESPN/Turner deal has all games set for big networks despite NBA |url=https://awfulannouncing.com/nhl/first-nhl-playoffs-espn-turner-big-networks-overflow.html |access-date=May 3, 2022 |website=Awful Announcing |date=May 2, 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last=Shapiro |first=Sean |title=NHL Playoffs: Where and how to watch on TV and streaming platforms in the U.S. |url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/3281530/2022/05/02/nhl-playoffs-viewing-guide-where-and-how-to-watch-playoff-hockey-on-tv-and-streaming-platforms-in-the-u-s/ |access-date=May 3, 2022 |website=TheAthletic.com |publisher=The Athletic Media Company |date=May 2, 2022}}</ref>

For the second consecutive year, the Final had an alternate telecast with commentary and analysis in [[American Sign Language]] for the benefit of the deaf community. It was available on Sportsnet+ in Canada and Max in the U.S.<ref>{{cite web |title='NHL in ASL' to return for 2025 Stanley Cup Final |url=https://www.nhl.com/news/nhl-in-asl-returns-for-2025-stanley-cup-final |access-date=December 23, 2025 |website=NHL.com |date=June 2, 2025}}</ref>
