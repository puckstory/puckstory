The '''2026 Stanley Cup Final''' was the [[Stanley Cup Final|championship series]] for the [[National Hockey League]]'s (NHL) [[2025–26 NHL season|2025–26 season]] and the culmination of the [[2026 Stanley Cup playoffs]] between the [[Eastern Conference (NHL)|Eastern Conference]] champion [[2025–26 Carolina Hurricanes season|Carolina Hurricanes]] and the [[Western Conference (NHL)|Western Conference]] champion [[2025–26 Vegas Golden Knights season|Vegas Golden Knights]]. The series began on June 2 and ended on June 14, with the Hurricanes defeating the Golden Knights four games to two, winning their second [[Stanley Cup]] championship and first since [[2006 Stanley Cup Final|2006]].

Carolina had [[home ice advantage]] in the series due to having a better regular season record. This was the first Final since {{scfy|2019}} to not feature a team from the state of [[Florida]], as the two-time defending champion [[Florida Panthers]] failed to qualify for the playoffs, and the [[Tampa Bay Lightning]] were eliminated by the [[Montreal Canadiens]] in the first round.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Long |first1=Corey |title=Lightning lose East 1st Round to Canadiens, Dobes' goaltending among factors |website=NHL.com |url=https://www.nhl.com/news/why-tampa-bay-lightning-are-eliminated-from-2025-2026-postseason |date=May 4, 2026 |access-date=June 21, 2026}}</ref>

==Paths to the Final==

===Carolina Hurricanes===
{{Main|2025–26 Carolina Hurricanes season}}
This was the third Stanley Cup Final for Carolina. Their first appearance in [[2002 Stanley Cup Final|2002]] was a loss to the [[Detroit Red Wings]] in five games. They won the Stanley Cup during their second appearance in {{scfy|2006}}, where they defeated the [[Edmonton Oilers]] in seven games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/2006/06/20/5497528/carolina-hurricanes-capture-stanley-cup|title=Carolina Hurricanes Capture Stanley Cup|last=Hochberg|first=Adam|date=June 20, 2006|website=NPR.org|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref>

During the offseason, Carolina traded [[Scott Morrow (ice hockey, born 2002)|Scott Morrow]] and two picks to the [[New York Rangers]] for defenceman [[K'Andre Miller]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/45635465/rangers-trade-kandre-miller-rival-hurricanes|title=New York Rangers trade K'Andre Miller to Carolina Hurricanes|last=Kaplan|first=Emily|date=July 1, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref> They also picked up forward [[Nikolaj Ehlers]] in free agency.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/7303916/2026/05/24/hurricanes-canadiens-nikolaj-ehlers-nhl-playoffs/|title=Nikolaj Ehlers is rewriting his career playoff narrative with the Hurricanes|last=Mirtle|first=James|date=May 24, 2026|newspaper=May 29, 2026|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref> Finally, they re-signed [[Logan Stankoven]] and [[Jackson Blake]] each to eight-year contracts.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.wral.com/sports/archive/22071443/|title=Hurricanes sign Logan Stankoven to eight-year contract extension|last=Fernandez|first=Louis|date=July 1, 2025|website=WRAL.com|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/45818950/hurricanes-lock-jackson-blake-8-year-45-million-deal|title=Hurricanes lock in Jackson Blake with 8-year, $45 million deal|via=Associated Press|date=July 24, 2025|website=ESPN.com|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref> Prior to the start of the season, they picked up goaltender [[Brandon Bussi]] on waivers from the [[Florida Panthers]], signing him to a three-year contract later in the season.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/47950322/hurricanes-sign-brandon-bussi-3-year-57m-extension|title=Hurricanes sign Brandon Bussi to 3-year, $5.7M extension|via=Associated Press|date=February 16, 2026|website=ESPN.com|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref> [[Sebastian Aho]] led the team in scoring with 80 points.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/carolina-top-line-of-aho-jarvis-svechnikov-renewed-force-in-nhl-east-final|title=Hurricanes top line a renewed force against Canadiens in Eastern Final|last=Benjamin|first=Amalie|date=May 29, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref>

The Hurricanes finished first in the [[Metropolitan Division (NHL)|Metropolitan Division]] and [[Eastern Conference (NHL)|Eastern Conference]] with 113 points via a {{win-loss record|w=53|l=22|otl=7}} record. In the playoffs, the Hurricanes completed back-to-back four-game sweeps against the [[Ottawa Senators]] and [[Philadelphia Flyers]] in the first and second rounds.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.ninertimes.com/sports/hurricanes-open-the-playoffs-with-back-to-back-sweeps/article_be2cd95f-c6de-4908-9198-3622f00214f0.html|title=Hurricanes open the playoffs with back-to-back sweeps|last=Sullivan|first=PJ|date=May 14, 2026|newspaper=Niner Times|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref> In their second consecutive Eastern Conference final, they defeated the [[Montreal Canadiens]] in five games. The Hurricanes entered the Final with one loss in the postseason, the fewest since the [[1975–76 Montreal Canadiens season|1975–76 Montreal Canadiens]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://nsjonline.com/article/2026/05/hurricanes-finish-off-canadiens-advance-to-stanley-cup-final-with-6-1-win/|title=Hurricanes finish off Canadiens, advance to Stanley Cup final with 6–1 win|date=May 30, 2026|newspaper=North State Journal|access-date=May 29, 2026}}</ref>

===Vegas Golden Knights===
{{Main|2025–26 Vegas Golden Knights season}}
This was the third Stanley Cup Final appearance for the Golden Knights. Their first appearance came during their inaugural season in [[2018 Stanley Cup Final|2018]], in which they lost to the [[Washington Capitals]] in five games.<ref>{{cite web |title=Capitals finish off Golden Knights in Game 5 to win Stanley Cup |url=https://www.sportsnet.ca/hockey/nhl/capitals-finish-off-golden-knights-game-5-win-stanley-cup/ |website=Sportsnet |publisher=Canadian Press |date=June 7, 2018 |access-date=June 21, 2026}}</ref> Their only Stanley Cup victory came in {{scfy|2023}}, where they defeated the [[Florida Panthers]] in five games.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/37846283/vegas-golden-knights-stanley-cup-champion-disruption-misfits-2023|title=How Golden Knights disrupted the NHL, won the Stanley Cup|last=Wyshynski|first=Greg|date=May 25, 2026|website=ESPN.com|access-date=May 25, 2026}}</ref>

During the offseason, the Golden Knights traded for defenceman [[Jérémy Lauzon]] and forward [[Colton Sissons]] from the [[Nashville Predators]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/nhl/story/_/id/45612849/golden-knights-deal-nicolas-hague-predators|title=Golden Knights deal Hague to Predators for Lauzon, Sissons|last=Wyshnyski|first=Greg|date=June 30, 2025|website=NHL.com|access-date=May 25, 2026}}</ref> They also acquired forward [[Mitch Marner]] from the [[Toronto Maple Leafs]], who was signed to an eight-year contract prior to his trade.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://apnews.com/article/mitch-marner-golden-knights-contract-e8f9aa4725812b29818c007dada6052b|title=All-Star winger Mitch Marner looks forward to fitting into family culture of Vegas Golden Knights|last=Ramirez|first=W. G.|date=July 1, 2025|website=APNews.com|access-date=May 25, 2026}}</ref> After three seasons away from the Golden Knights, including two with Carolina, defenseman [[Dylan Coghlan]] returned for a second stint on a one-year contract.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nhl.com/goldenknights/news/vegas-golden-knights-announce-roster-transactions-x3533 |title=Vegas Golden Knights Announce Roster Transactions |website=NHL.com |date=July 1, 2025 |access-date=July 1, 2025}}</ref> They additionally re-signed forwards [[Brandon Saad]] and [[Reilly Smith]] to one-year contracts.<ref>{{cite In the first period of game two, [[Mitch Marner]] lobbed the puck out of the Vegas defensive zone and [[Brett Howden]] picked up the puck and scored to give the Golden Knights a 1–0 lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-howden-scores-goal-against-frederik-andersen-6397591799112|title=Howden strikes first in Game 2|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> After an unsuccessful power play for the Golden Knights in the second period, Howden scored again receiving assists from [[Ivan Barbashev]] and [[Noah Hanifin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-howden-scores-goal-against-frederik-andersen-6397594050112|title=Howden scores again|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> In the third period, the Hurricanes cut Vegas's lead to one as [[Logan Stankoven]] scored an unassisted goal, shooting the puck and deflecting past goaltender [[Carter Hart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-stankoven-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6397601093112|title=VGK@CAR: Stankoven scores goal against Carter Hart|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> They tied the game just over two minutes later as [[William Carrier]] passed the puck to [[Mark Jankowski]] whose wrist shot got past Hart.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-jankowski-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6397600403112|title=VGK@CAR: Jankowski scores goal against Carter Hart|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> With five minutes remaining in the game, the referee blew the whistle to stop play though the puck went into the Carolina net. Vegas challenged the play that no goaltender interference occurred to cause the whistle stoppage. The challenge was unsuccessful. On the ensuing power play, Carolina defenceman [[Shayne Gostisbehere]] shot the puck through traffic and [[Jordan Staal]] deflected the puck into the net, erasing the two-goal deficit to lead 3–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-staal-scores-ppg-against-carter-hart-6397601525112|title=VGK@CAR: Staal scores PPG against Carter Hart|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> With an empty net and an extra attacker for Vegas, [[Mark Stone]] scored to tie the game 3–3, sending it to overtime.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-stone-scores-goal-against-frederik-andersen-6397603327112|title=Stone evens it on the backhand|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref> In overtime, [[Tomáš Hertl]] took a tripping penalty and on the Carolina power play, [[Seth Jarvis]] scored to give the Hurricanes a 4–3 victory.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/vegas-golden-knights-carolina-hurricanes-stanley-cup-final-game-2-recap-june-4-2026|title=Jarvis scores in OT, Hurricanes top Golden Knights in Game 2 to even Cup Final|last=Dusterberg|first=Kurt|date=June 4, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 4, 2026}}</ref>

The first period of game three remained scoreless with the shots favoring Carolina 7–2. In the second period, two goals by Vegas were challenged, first for an offside call and the second on goaltender interference. Both resulted in the goals being called back. The Hurricanes took a "too many on the ice" penalty, and on the power play for Vegas, [[Tomáš Hertl]] scored. [[Mitch Marner]] then scored as his shot was deflected off of a Carolina player's stick.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-marner-scores-goal-against-frederik-andersen-6397782434112|title=Marner banks puck home|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> Marner scored his second goal less than four minutes later, shooting the puck on his backhand past goaltender [[Frederik Andersen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-marner-scores-goal-against-frederik-andersen-6397783608112|title=Marner's second of the period|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> Marner completed the [[Hat-trick#Hockey|hat trick]] as his third goal came from a slap shot.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/gamecenter/car-vs-vgk/2026/06/06/2025030413|title=Marner finishes the hat trick|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> His hat trick goal was the fastest in Stanley Cup Final history, scoring all three goals within 6:10.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reviewjournal.com/sports/goldenknights/mitch-marner-continues-his-playoff-dominance-with-record-setting-hat-trick-3834460/?utm_campaign=widget&utm_medium=topnews&utm_source=sports_goldenknights&utm_term=Mitch%20Marner%20continues%20his%20playoff%20dominance%20with%20record-setting%20hat%20trick|title=Mitch Marner continues his playoff dominance with record-setting hat trick|last=Hill|first=Adam|date=June 6, 2026|newspaper=Las Vegas Review-Journal|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> The Hurricanes pulled Andersen after the end of the second period, opting to put in backup goaltender [[Brandon Bussi]]. Marner had a chance to make it 5–0 on a penalty shot, but was stopped by Bussi. The Hurricanes broke the shutout as they scored three goals within 39 seconds to cut the deficit to one goal. [[Jordan Martinook]] scored first firing a wrist shot past [[Carter Hart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-martinook-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6397785354112|title=Martinook puts Hurricanes on the board|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> [[Taylor Hall]] scored second as [[Sebastian Aho]] sent a backhand pass to Hall scoring to put it to 4–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-hall-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6397784491112|title=Aho sets Hall up down low|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> Captain [[Jordan Staal]] then scored off a face-off, making the score 4–3 for Vegas.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-staal-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6397785860112|title=Staal finishes off the draw|date=June 6, 2026|website=NHL.com|access-date=June 6, 2026}}</ref> With 1:42 left in the game, on the power play, [[Andrei Svechnikov]] scored to tie the game 4–4. The game went into the double-overtime period, wherein [[Shea Theodore]] scored to give Vegas a 5–4 victory and spoil Carolina's comeback.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/carolina-hurricanes-vegas-golden-knights-stanley-cup-final-game-3-recap-june-6-2026|title=Golden Knights recover for 2OT victory against Hurricanes in Game 3 of Cup Final|last=Rosen|first=Dan|date=June 7, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 7, 2026}}</ref>

[[Jackson Blake]] made it 2–0 for Carolina just as the Golden Knights' penalty expired, accepting a pass from [[Taylor Hall]] and firing the puck into the net.<ref>{{cite captain [[Mark Stone]] put Vegas on the board, faking a slap shot and tucking the puck behind goaltender [[Brandon Bussi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-stone-scores-goal-against-brandon-bussi-6398024290112|title=Stone starts off Golden Knights|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> Later in the first period, Vegas was given a penalty for too many men on the ice; Carolina captain [[Jordan Staal]] then scored to make it 3–1.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-staal-scores-ppg-against-carter-hart-6398022859112|title=Staal buries power-play goal|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> [[Brayden McNabb]] attempted a buzzer-beater, putting the puck into the net just as the first period ended, but on video replay the goal was waived off as it had not completely crossed the line prior to the time expiration.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.espn.com/watch/player/_/id/49014737|title=Golden Knights' goal at the buzzer waived off|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> In the second period, [[William Karlsson]] scored Vegas's second goal of the game, taking a pass from [[Rasmus Andersson]] firing a wrist shot past Bussi.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-karlsson-scores-goal-against-brandon-bussi-6398026272112|title=Karlsson snaps one home|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> The Golden Knights tied up the score 3–3 during a two-on-one rush led by [[Colton Sissons]] and [[Brett Howden]], the latter of which scored.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-howden-scores-goal-against-brandon-bussi-6398028367112|title=Howden ties it up|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> In the third period, Vegas gave up the puck in their defensive zone, and while falling down, Staal scored to give the Hurricanes the lead once more, making it 4–3.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-staal-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6398034086112|title=Staal lights lamp again|date=June 9, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 9, 2026}}</ref> [[Nikolaj Ehlers]] made it 5–3 with an [[empty net goal]], tying up the series 2–2.<ref>{{cite In game five, Vegas started the scoring on the power play. As Carolina forward [[Nikolaj Ehlers]] was in the penalty box for shooting the puck over the glass, [[Pavel Dorofeyev]] scored for the Golden Knights to make it 1–0. Carolina tied it 1–1 later in the first period as Ehlers passed the puck to captain [[Jordan Staal]] who shot the puck past goaltender [[Carter Hart]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-staal-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6398198770112|title=Staal's tip-in goal|date=June 11, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 11, 2026}}</ref> Carolina gained the lead in the second period. [[Sebastian Aho]] then made it 3–1 with [[Sean Walker (ice hockey)|Sean Walker]] and [[Jordan Martinook]] both assisting. In the third period, the Hurricanes made it 4–1 on the power play as [[Shayne Gostisbehere]] passed to an open Ehlers who spun around and backhanded the puck to Svechnikov who scored.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/vgk-car-svechnikov-scores-ppg-against-carter-hart-6398205026112|title=Svechnikov's second PPG|date=June 11, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 11, 2026}}</ref> The Golden Knights cut the deficit to two goals as Dorofeyev scored his second of the game off of a rebound created by [[Shea Theodore]]. In game six, Carolina defenceman [[Jaccob Slavin]] dished a long pass to [[Taylor Hall]] who scored to give the Hurricanes a 1–0 lead.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://nhl.com/video/car-vgk-hall-scores-goal-against-carter-hart-6398421474112|title=Hall strikes first in Game 6|date=June 14, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 14, 2026}}</ref> Goaltender [[Brandon Bussi]] kept the Hurricanes at a 1–0 lead through the first period, stopping 11 shots from the Golden Knights. In the second period, the Hurricanes made it 2–0 with [[Jackson Blake]] scoring. The Golden Knights were held to three shots for the remainder of the period. In the third period, the Golden Knights pulled their goaltender, but [[Nikolaj Ehlers]] sealed Carolina's Stanley Cup victory with an empty net goal to make it 3–0.<ref name="G6">{{cite web|url=https://www.nhl.com/news/carolina-hurricanes-vegas-golden-knights-stanley-cup-final-game-6-recap-june-14-2026|title=Hurricanes win Stanley Cup with Game 6 shutout against Golden Knights|last=Rosen|first=Dan|date=June 14, 2026|website=NHL.com|accessdate=June 14, 2026}}</ref>

Hurricanes captain [[Jordan Staal]] was awarded the [[Conn Smythe Trophy]] as most valuable player of the playoffs.<ref name="G6"/> [[Rod Brind'Amour]] also became the first person to win the Stanley Cup as both a former player and coach for the same team since [[Toe Blake]] in {{scfy|1956}}.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/athletic/7361618/2026/06/15/rod-brindamour-stanley-cup-hurricanes-coach/|title=Rod Brind'Amour leads the Hurricanes to a Stanley Cup — this time from behind the bench|last=LeBrun|first=Pierre|date=June 15, 2026|newspaper=The New York Times|accessdate=June 15, 2026}}</ref>
