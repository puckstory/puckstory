The '''1925 Stanley Cup Final''' saw the [[Western Canada Hockey League]] (WCHL) champion [[Victoria Cougars]] defeat the [[National Hockey League]] (NHL) champion [[1924–25 Montreal Canadiens season|Montreal Canadiens]] three games to one in a best-of-five game series. The Canadiens were substitute NHL representatives, as the final series to decide the NHL champion was not played.

The Cougars were the last non-NHL team to win the Cup as the WCHL (renamed the Western Hockey League for the [[1925–26 WHL season|1925–26 season]]) folded after [[1926 Stanley Cup Finals|1926]], leaving the Stanley Cup to become the NHL's ''de facto'' championship trophy.  The Cougars would also be the last team based west of [[Chicago]] to win the Cup until the [[Edmonton Oilers]] won the trophy in [[1984 Stanley Cup Finals|1984]]. These were also the last Stanley Cup Finals games to be played in Western Canada until the [[Vancouver Canucks]] qualified for the [[1982 Stanley Cup Finals|1982 Finals]]. Games one, three, and four were held in Victoria. Game two, held in Vancouver, was the last neutral site game in Stanley Cup Finals history that did not involve the [[New York Rangers]] until the [[2020 Stanley Cup Finals]].

==Path to the Finals==
{{Main|1924–25 NHL season|1924–25 WCHL season}}

Prior to the season, the [[Pacific Coast Hockey Association]] (PCHA) folded and two of its teams, the Cougars and the [[Vancouver Maroons]] joined the WCHL. Victoria finished the [[1924–25 WCHL season|1924–25 WCHL regular season]] in third place, but eventually upset the [[Calgary Tigers]] in the two-game total goals WCHL championship series by a combined score of 3–1.

Meanwhile, the Canadiens also finished the [[1924–25 NHL season|NHL regular season]] in third place. In the NHL playoffs, Montreal went on to beat the second place [[Toronto St. Patricks]], 5–2, in a two-game total goals series. The winner of that series was to go on and play the first place [[Hamilton Tigers]]. However, the Tigers were suspended after Hamilton players staged a [[Strike action|strike]] in an attempt to receive more compensation because the league extended the regular season from 24 to 30 games. As a result, the Canadiens were declared the 1924–25 NHL champions.

==Game summaries==
With the demise of the PCHA, the Stanley Cup playoffs reverted to a single best-of-five series to determine the champion. However, the Cup Finals still annually rotated between the east and the west, and thus all of the games in the 1925 Finals were played on the West Coast. Games one, three, and four were played at the 4,200 seat [[Patrick Arena]] in Victoria; game two was played at the [[Denman Arena]] in Vancouver.<ref>{{cite news |newspaper=Montreal Gazette |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=Fr8DH2VBP9sC&dat=19250319&printsec=frontpage |title=First Game Saturday |date=March 19, 1925 |page=14 |access-date=September 16, 2010}}</ref> The decision to use the larger Denman Arena (10,500 seats) for game two was based on the huge demand for tickets.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20081024202544/http://www.salmonkings.com/the-team/hockey-history/victoria.html "Colorful Canadiens"] Victoria Hockey History. Retrieved 2010-09-16</ref> The Cougars jumped to a two games to none series lead with 5–2 and 3–1 victories, but the Canadiens won game three, 4–2. In game four, [[Gizzy Hart]] scored the game-winning goal in Victoria's 6–1 win to clinch the Cup.<ref>[https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=Fr8DH2VBP9sC&dat=19250331&printsec=frontpage “Canadiens Leave Stanley Cup With Victoria Cougars”] The Montreal Gazette newspaper – March 31, 1925, page 14. Retrieved 2010-09-16</ref>

Cougars goaltender [[Hap Holmes]] recorded a 2.00 goal-against average for the series. [[Jack Walker (ice hockey)|Jack Walker]] led Victoria in goals with four, while [[Frank Fredrickson]] scored three. Overall, eight different player combining for the Cougars' 16 goals.

==Stanley Cup engraving==
The 1925 Stanley Cup was presented to Cougars captain Clem Loughlin by the trophy's trustee [[William Foran]], following the Cougars 6–1 win over the Canadiens in game four.
