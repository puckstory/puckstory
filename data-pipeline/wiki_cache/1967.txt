The '''1967 Stanley Cup Final''' was the [[Stanley Cup Final|championship series]] of the [[National Hockey League]]'s (NHL) [[1966–67 NHL season|1966–67 season]], and the culmination of the [[1967 Stanley Cup playoffs]]. A best-of-seven series, it was contested between the [[1966–67 Montreal Canadiens season|Montreal Canadiens]] and the [[1966–67 Toronto Maple Leafs season|Toronto Maple Leafs]]. This was the fifth and most recent Cup Final Meeting in the history of the [[Canadiens-Maple Leafs rivalry]]. The Maple Leafs defeated the Canadiens in six games to win their thirteenth and most recent [[Stanley Cup]] championship.

To date, this is Toronto's last appearance in the Stanley Cup Final and they have [[List of NHL franchise post-season droughts#Stanley Cup droughts|the longest-active championship drought]] in the NHL. The 1967 series was also the last Stanley Cup Final in the [[Original Six]] era. This was also the last all-Canadian Final series until [[1986 Stanley Cup Final|1986]], the last Stanley Cup Final to be played in Ontario until [[2007 Stanley Cup Final|2007]], and the last championship series to feature a Toronto-based team until the [[Toronto Blue Jays|Blue Jays]] made the [[1992 World Series]].

==Paths to the Final==
{{main|1967 Stanley Cup playoffs}}
This was the last Stanley Cup before the 1967 expansion, which meant there were only two rounds and three series in total played in the playoffs. Montreal defeated New York to advance to the Final and Toronto defeated Chicago.

==Game summaries==

The average age of the Leafs' players was 31, the oldest lineup to win the Cup.<ref>{{cite book|title=Stanley Cup|pages=42}}</ref> [[Johnny Bower]] was 42 and [[Allan Stanley]] was 41. Toronto won the series in six games, with [[Dave Keon]] won the [[Conn Smythe Trophy]].

In the sixth game, Johnny Bower returned to the line-up as back up for Toronto. [[Gump Worsley]] would also replace [[Rogie Vachon]] for Montreal, who had been pulled in game five. [[Jim Pappin]] scored his seventh goal of the playoffs and Sawchuk stopped 40 shots helping Toronto win the Cup. Pappin had four goals and four assists in the final series. Captain [[George Armstrong (ice hockey)|George Armstrong]] scored the 3–1 empty-net insurance goal to put game six out of reach.

==Stanley Cup engraving==
The 1967 Stanley Cup was presented to Maple Leafs captain George Armstrong by [[NHL Commissioner|NHL President]] [[Clarence Campbell]] following the Maple Leafs 3–1 win over the Canadiens in Game Six.

The following Maple Leafs players and staff had their names engraved on the Stanley Cup.

|engraving-notes=
* The first "K" in Peter Stemkowski's name was engraved backwards.
* Stafford Smythe's name was misspelled  C. ST FFORD SMYTHE PRESIDENT, missing an "A". Both mistakes were corrected on the Replica Cup created in 1992–93.
* The job title for John Anderson was misspelled BUISS MANN (sic)
* Leonard "Red" Kelly won the Stanley Cup 8 times.  He was engraved as Leonard Kelly in 1954, 1955, 1962, 1963, and 1964.  Kelly was engraved as Red Kelly in 1950, 1952, and 1967.
* <sup>{{note|A|A}}</sup> #25 [[Milan Marcetta]] (C) played 3 games in the Final, and #24 [[Aut Erickson]] (D) played 1 game in the Final, qualifying to be on the Stanley Cup engraving.  Neither player played a game for Toronto in the regular season.
*[[Johnny Bower]] (G) was 42 years old when he won his fourth and final Stanley Cup.

Left off the Stanley Cup for playing in the minors during the playoffs.
* #24 [[John Brenneman]] (LW) played 41 games, but played in the minors during the playoffs
* #19 [[Kent Douglas]] (D) played 39 games, but played in the minors during the playoffs
* #1-#30 [[Bruce Gamble]] (G) played 23 regular-season games, dressed for 35, but was playing in the minors during the playoffs.
* #1 [[Al Smith (ice hockey)|Al Smith]]^ (G) was dressed in the finals, due to Johnny Bowers missing games 4 and 5 (of 6) in the finals due to injury. Smith's name was left off the Cup because he only played 1 regular-season game and did not play in the playoffs. Al Smith qualified to be on the Cup because he was dressed in the series. 
* Dr. Hugh Symthe (Team Physician) – Also left (His name is on cup 1942 with Toronto as mascot).}}
